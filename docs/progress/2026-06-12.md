- The −0.26 in CV (v4) translated into −0.57 on LB: **the text signal is more valuable on the
  late-year-weighted test set than CV indicates.**
- Multi-seed averaging: the CV difference turned out to be pure noise. The text ceiling is confirmed.
- **Pseudo-labeling lesson (important):** feeding test predictions into training makes the OOF
  structurally optimistic (information from every validation fold leaks through the pseudo-labels). We saw CV 72.68,
  LB came in at 83.22. **Do not trust any CV that contains test-derived labels.** exp017 was removed from the blend.

## 3. "Are we missing something simple?" audit (4 hypotheses, 4 rejections)

| hypothesis | result |
|---|---|
| Digit/score leakage inside the text | ❌ zero of the 10,000 texts contain a digit |
| Train–test row/text duplication | ❌ zero overlap |
| Target is discrete (rounding/banding trick) | ❌ 4402 distinct continuous values |
| Ceiling share is miscalibrated | ❌ expected test 100% share 0.095, our pred≥95 share 0.093; consistent year-by-year |

Conclusion: the 0.94 MSE between us and the leaders is a fine-tuning difference; there is no structural miss.
